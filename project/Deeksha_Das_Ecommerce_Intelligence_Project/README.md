E-commerce Intelligence: Customer 360 Analytics Framework
By Deeksha Das

1. Business Problem
E-commerce data is heavily fragmented across multiple systems (orders, payments, reviews, catalogs). This project unifies these sources into a single "Customer 360 View" to identify revenue drivers, map customer behavior, and fix operational bottlenecks.

2. Dataset & Methodology
The Data: Integrated 9 relational datasets (customers, orders, items, products, sellers, payments, reviews, locations, category translations) by mapping primary and foreign keys.

The Pipeline: Collecting Data ➔ Data Cleaning ➔ Data Integration ➔ Feature Engineering ➔ EDA & Data Visualization ➔ Strategic Recommendations.

3. Key Findings
Revenue Cash Cows: Health & Beauty, Watches & Gifts, and Bed, Bath & Table generate the absolute highest revenue.

Customer Behavior: Sales are heavily concentrated in Southeast Brazil (SP, RJ, MG). The business suffers from a massive "One-and-Done" problem, with most users making a single credit card purchase and never returning.

Operational Inefficiencies: Delivery time directly dictates customer satisfaction. Orders arriving 15+ days late crash to an average review score of 1.71, while early deliveries boost scores to 4.22.

4. Strategic Recommendations
Targeted Logistics Optimization: Implement tiered SLAs that algorithmically prioritize "Fast Track" sellers (under 7-day delivery) and penalize sellers causing severe delays.

Regional Warehousing: Expand physical fulfillment centers in the high-volume states of SP, RJ, and MG to guarantee early deliveries and 5-star reviews.

The "Loyalty Loop": Combat the "One-and-Done" problem by sending automated discount emails 30 days post-purchase to drastically increase Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

Dynamic Free Shipping: Set free shipping thresholds just 15% higher than the current Average Order Value (AOV) to mathematically incentivize users to add a second item to their cart.
